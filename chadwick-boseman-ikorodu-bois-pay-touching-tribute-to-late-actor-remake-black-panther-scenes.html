Chadwick Boseman: Ikorodu Bois pay touching tribute to late actor, remake Black Panther scenes

- Talented Nigerian youngsters known as Ikorodu Bois have again gained international recognition on social media

- The young lads, just like many others, were touched by the death of American actor, Chadwick Boseman

- The Ikorodu Bois paid a touching tribute to the late actor by remaking scenes from the popular superhero movie, Black Panther

Some talented Nigerian youngsters known as the Ikorodu Bois have touched many with their emotional tribute to late American actor, Chadwick Boseman.

The news of Boseman’s death was revealed on August 29, 2020. It was gathered that the American star died after his long battle with colon cancer.

The Ikorodu Bois became a household name on Nigerian social media space due to their creativity in remaking popular videos with materials readily available in their vicinity.

The young lads, just like many people all over the world were touched by the sudden death of Boseman and they resorted to eulogizing him in the only way they knew.

The Ikorodu Bois paid a tribute to the actor by remaking scenes from the popular superhero movie called Black Panther.

The boys with their friends acted the role of T’Challa, his father, Mbaku, to name a few of the characters. Their short video was well received by fans in Nigeria and overseas.
