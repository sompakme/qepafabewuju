What Time Does Shabbat End NYC

Shabbat Shalom from the Big Apple!

Hey there, friends! Wondering when you can finally dig into that delicious challah you've been eyeing all weekend? If you're in the NYC area, fret no more, because I've got you covered on the official (well, almost official ) end time for Shabbat.

Hold on to your hats, because tonight's Havdalah is at...

(drumroll please)

8:33 pm! ⭐️

That means you can get ready to say goodbye to the tranquility of Shabbat and dive back into the hustle and bustle of NYC (but maybe not too fast – ease back into that email inbox, y'all).

Fun Fact: Everything is Bigger in New York...Including Shabbat Dinner Portions?

While I can't vouch for the portion sizes at your Shabbat dinner table, I can tell you that NYC has a pretty vibrant Jewish community. From bustling synagogues in Brooklyn to trendy kosher restaurants in the Lower East Side, there's something for everyone.

Friendly Reminder: Don't Forget the Challah (and Maybe an Eruv if You're Feeling Fancy)

Speaking of challah, don't forget to grab a slice (or two, or three...) before Havdalah. Also, a quick heads up for those who are super observant: if you're planning on carrying that challah a bit further than your doorstep (maybe to a friend's place for a post-Shabbat hangout?), you might need an eruv. But hey, don't worry about the details for now – just enjoy the end of Shabbat!

Alright folks, that's all from your friendly neighborhood Shabbat timekeeper. Go forth and celebrate!
