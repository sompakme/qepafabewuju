Demi Lovatos Glass-Like Skin Is Thanks to Innisfree's K-Beauty Serum

Publish date: 2024-07-25

In light of the current heat wave engulfing New York City, I’ve swapped my rich, emollient moisturizer for a lightweight, cooling water cream — specifically, the Green Tea Hyaluronic Acid Moisturizer from Korean beauty brand Innisfree. I’ve been loving its glow-inducing effects and skin-soothing feel — and with Demi Lovato recently donning its serum counterpart, the Innisfree Green Tea Hyaluronic Acid Hydrating Serum, I’m absolutely looking to try it out. Luckily for me — and anyone else craving dewy skin — the serum is accessible at Amazon for $30.

The Innisfree Green Tea Hyaluronic Acid Hydrating Serum is a skin-quenching, lightweight prep step before moisturizer that provides both antioxidant protection and intense, dewy, skin-plumping hydration thanks to two key ingredients: hyaluronic acid and green tea. Formulated for all skin types, it’s non-comedogenic, fast absorbing, and plays well with other products. Plus, as evidenced by Lovato’s dramatic, perfectly smooth makeup look, the serum layers beautifully under foundation and other ensuing cosmetics. 

Lovato donned Innisfree’s serum in Brazil recently, where it functioned as the first step in her pre-performance skincare prep. Jill Powell, the makeup artist who created Lovato’s glossy complexion, spilled the exact products she used to enhance Lovato’s skin to peak plumpness via an Instagram post. “I literally get so many DMs about this look,” she shared in her caption — and after ogling at the images, I can see why. Lovato’s skin looked poreless, smooth, and plumper than plump. Her finished look could only be described as quintessential glass skin.

The serum’s key ingredients explain its plumping, glow-inducing effects. Hailed as a hydrating superhero, hyaluronic acid is a water-loving molecule. Found naturally in the body, it’s also in myriad skincare formulas thanks to its ability to quench dry skin, plump fine lines, and generally deliver a dewy, glowy complexion in mere moments. 

Green tea extract is an antioxidant-rich ingredient that promotes youthful-looking skin. Specifically, it fends off skin-aging free radicals found in the environment. Green tea extract is also anti-inflammatory, per dermatologist Dr. Stacy Chimento Capote, M.D., who previously told InStyle it’s “amazing” in this regard. Moreover, she shared, "Green tea soothes the skin” and “reduces sebum production,” making it wonderfully calming for sensitive types, and potentially pore-clearing for those prone to accumulating blemish-causing debris. As with green tea you’d drink, the skincare extract version “also contains tannins and caffeine,” Dr. Chimento shared, “which shrinks blood vessels and reduces puffiness." Isn’t that what we’d all like on early mornings?

For plump, glass-like skin à la Demi Lovato, shop the Innisfree Green Tea Serum, which is sure to enhance any existing skincare routine with its ultra-hydrating, plumping prowess.
