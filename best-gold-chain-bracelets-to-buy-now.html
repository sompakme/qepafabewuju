Best gold chain bracelets to buy now

Publish date: 2024-09-05

We earn a commission for products purchased through some links in this article.

Alighieri

Each piece from London-based jewellery Alighieri draws inspiration from the work of one of Italy's finest poets, Dante Alighieri, and his 14th-century work, The Divine Comedy. This chunky gold-brass chain features a beautiful silver charm, giving it that extra level of beauty.

Fope

The Eka collection from luxury Italian jewellery brand Fope is inspired by the concept of flexibility and is based on one of Fope's original styles from the 1950s. Its beauty lies in its simplicity, finished with a subtle clasp with a tiny diamond gemstone.

Laura Lombardi

Laura Lombardi

A native New Yorker, Lombardi's linear designs have plenty of celebrity admirers, including Tracee Ellis Ross, Emma Watson and Jorja Smith. Use her chunky 'Rafaella' bracelet to add a touch of glitz to your favourite denim outfit.

Vrai

Vrai is a master of delicate designs that sit effortlessly on the wrist. The jewellery brand's 'Bezel Station' bracelet is very special, as it features three diamonds that catch the light perfectly.

Monica Vinader

Monica Vinader

Pearl fans listen up. This boxy chain bracelet from Monica Vinader comes with a baroque pearl pendant for a little romantic flourish. We think this design would look effortlessly chic when worn with a timeless white shirt and jeans.

Missoma

This chunky gold-plated bracelet has a flattened chain and T-bar clasp in a nod to 1980s styling. The beauty of gold bracelets is that anything goes – wear them with a mix of shapes and sizes for a statement look.

Louise Olsen

Louise Olsen

Australian designer Louise Olsen creates fluid, sculptural pieces that are as much about art as they are about adornment. Great for those who want something a little out there, which can still be worn every day.

Versace

Versace's gold bracelet features the brand's 'Grecomania' charm and its iconic Medusa head motif. This design would look just as good worn alone, or stacked up with other statement pieces.

Maria Black

Maria Black

Lovers of Scandi-chic will adore this sleek, minimalist chain from Danish jewellery designer, Maria Black. The flat design means you can wear it day after day, without it feeling like too much.

Tiffany & Co

Tiffany & Co

In 1974, Tiffany & Co approached style maven and former model Elsa Peretti to create a modern iteration of diamond jewellery. The result was a length of chain with twinkling diamonds interspersed at varying stages, which her fashion designer friend Halston later christened 'Diamonds by the Yard'. It's been a bestseller ever since.

Isabel Marant

Isabel Marant

Take a bolder approach with Isabel Marant's chunky chain bracelet – this is a great statement piece that looks spectacular worn alone, or if you really want to go for it, team with the matching choker necklace.

Cartier

We all know how iconic Cartier's Love bracelet is – thanks to its easy-to-wear nature and romantic meaning – so we love this fun delicate twist on the style that's a subtle nod to the timeless design.
