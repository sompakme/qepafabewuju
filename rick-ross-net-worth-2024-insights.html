Who is Rick Ross?

Rick Ross, born William Leonard Roberts II on January 28, 1976, in Clarksdale, Mississippi, and raised in Carol City, Florida, is a prominent American rapper, songwriter, entrepreneur, and record executive. Known for his husky voice and vivid storytelling, Ross rose to fame in the mid-2000s with hits like “Hustlin'” and albums such as “Port of Miami” and “Trilla.”

He is also the founder of the record label Maybach Music Group, which has been home to several successful artists. Beyond music, Ross has diversified his career into ventures including authoring a memoir, owning Wingstop franchises, and investing in real estate.
