30 Best Ted Lasso Quotes The Hollywood Reporter

Publish date: 2024-07-06

AFC Richmond doesn’t always bring it on the field. But what the heroes of Ted Lasso lack in a winning record, they more than make up for with winning dialogue.

From series star Jason Sudeikis and fellow creators Bill Lawrence, Brendan Hunt and Joe Kelly, Ted Lasso deserves its reputation as one of the most quotable shows of the modern television age. It’s brimming with enough quips to match Coach Lasso’s infectious enthusiasm and a veritable stadium-sized amount of heartfelt sentiments — so many you couldn’t fit them all as Post-its on a wall. 

Rounding up the best Lasso quotes is not as daunting a task as the Bantr-sponsored Richmond’s quest to “win the whole fucking thing,” but it’s daunting all the same. And yet, for all who dare to try, it’s best to keep Ted’s simplest advice in mind: “Believe.”

Celebrating the Emmy-winning comedy’s March 15 season three debut, The Hollywood Reporter offers up the 30 best quotes from the wide world of Ted Lasso, presented as superlatives.

  • Most Iconic Mantra

    “Football is life.” — Dani Rojas, various episodes

  • Second Most Iconic Mantra

    “Be a goldfish.” — Ted Lasso, Season 1 Episode 2: “Biscuits”

  • Most Underrated Mantra

    “I’m a strong and capable man. I’m not a piece of shit.” — Colin Hughes, Season 2 Episode 6: “The Signal”

  • Best Therapeutic Advice

    “The truth will set you free… but first, it will piss you off.” — Dr. Sharon Fieldstone, Season 2 Episode 7: “Headspace,” and Season 2 Episode 12: “Inverting the Pyramid of Success”

  • Best Career Advice

    “A good mentor hopes you move on. A great mentor knows you will.” — Leslie Higgins, Season 2 Episode 12: “Inverting the Pyramid of Success”

  • Most Self-Aware Quote

    “I can diagnose myself in a heartbeat. I thought being invulnerable would protect me, so I pushed people away for years, leading me directly to my greatest fear: being alone. Big whoop.” — Rebecca Welton (pictured with Leslie), Season 2 Episode 2: “Lavender”

  • Second Most Self-Aware Quote

    “​​Coach, I’m me. Why would I want to be anything else?” — Jamie Tartt, Season 1 Episode 2: “Biscuits”

  • Most Accurate Assessment of Ice Cream

    “It’s kinda like seeing Billy Joel perform live. Never disappoints.” — Ted Lasso, Season 1 Episode 9: “All Apologies”

  • Best Way to Introduce New Friends

    “Congratulations, you both just met a cool person!” — Ted Lasso, Season 1 Episode 3: “Trent Crimm: The Independent”

  • Best Romantic Advice

    “You deserve someone who will make you feel like you’ve been struck by lightning. Don’t you dare settle for fine.” — Roy Kent, Season 2 Episode 1: “Goodbye Earl”

  • Best Star Wars Reference

    “Old people are so wise. They’re like Tall Yodas.” — Jamie Tartt (pictured), Season 2 Episode 2: “Lavender”

  • Best Back to the Future Reference

    “Ronald Reagan?? The actor???” — Coach Beard, Season 1 Episode 5: “Tan Lines”

  • Best Jerry Maguire Reference

    “You had me at Coach.” — Roy Kent, Season 2 Episode 5: “Rainbow”

  • Least Expected Video Game Reference

    “[Keeley, (pictured with Rebecca)] told me to expect a mustachioed surprise that would anger me. I thought it was gonna be Wario or my great aunt Natalie.” — Roy Kent, Season 2 Episode 5: “Rainbow”

  • Best Supernatural Action Plan

    “If we see each other in our dreams, let’s goof around a little bit, pretend like we don’t know each other.” — Ted Lasso, Season 1 Episode 1: “Pilot”

  • Best of Coach Beard's Uses of the Word "Baby"

    “Horticulture, baby!” — Coach Beard, Season 2 Episode 12: “Inverting the Pyramid of Success”

  • Best Nate Shelley Quote That’s a Little Less Funny Now

    “It’s Shelley and you know it, you dithering kestrel!” — Nate (pictured, with Ted Lasso), Season 2 Episode 5: “Rainbow”

  • Most Charming Sam Obisanya Moment

    “There’s something I should warn you of: I’m only going to get more wonderful.” — Sam Obisanya, Season 2 Episode 10: “No Weddings and a Funeral”

  • Best Bit of Coaching

    “I think that you might be so sure that you’re one in a million, that sometimes you forget that out there you’re just one in 11.” — Ted Lasso, Season 1 Episode 2: “Biscuits”

  • Best Coaching with an Unexpected Pop Culture Twist

    “We’re gonna call this drill The Exorcist, ’cause it’s all about controlling possession.” — Ted Lasso, Season 1 Episode 2: “Biscuits”

  • Best Use of a Walt Whitman Quote

    “‘Be curious, not judgmental.’ I like that.” — Ted Lasso, Season 1 Episode 8: “The Diamond Dogs”

  • Best Chant (Without Expletives)

    “We’re Richmond ’til we die, we’re Richmond ’til we die! We know we are, we’re sure we are, we’re Richmond ’til we die!” — Everyone, various episodes

  • Best Chant (Now With Expletives)

    “He’s here, he’s there, he’s every fucking where! Roy Kent! Roy Kent!” — Everyone, various episodes (Pictured: Roy Kent with Coach Beard and Ted Lasso)

  • Most Subdued Moment of Panic

    “I never know how to react when a grown man beatboxes in front of me.” — Keeley Jones, Season 1 Episode 1: “Pilot”

  • Most Unexpected Expression of Vulnerability

    “It hurt my … feeling.” — Roy Kent, Season 2 Episode 12: “Inverting the Pyramid of Success”

  • Greatest Admission of Defeat

    “If the Lasso way is wrong, it’s hard to imagine being right.” — Trent Crimm, Season 1 Episode 3: “Trent Crimm: The Independent”

  • Best Eulogy

    “This is my cat’s collar. She was a faithful companion for 20 years. I’m gonna miss you, Cindy Clawford.” — Leslie Higgins, Season 1 Episode 6: “Two Aces”

  • Best Quote to Understand the Whole Show

    “I think things come into our lives to help us get from one place to a better one.” — Ted Lasso, Season 2 Episode 1: “Goodbye Earl”

  • Most Iconic Quote in a Single Word

    “Believe.” — The Richmond Locker Room (pictured), various episodes

  • Second Most Iconic Quote in a Single Word

    “Fuck!” — Roy Kent, various episodes
