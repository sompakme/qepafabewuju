Aron Ralston’s Early Life and Career

Aron Ralston was born and raised in Marion, Ohio. He developed a love for the outdoors at a young age, which later played a significant role in shaping his career. After graduating from Carnegie Mellon University with a degree in mechanical engineering, Ralston moved to Arizona and began working for Intel. However, his passion for outdoor adventures led him to quit his job and pursue a life of climbing and hiking.

The Incident that Changed His Life

In 2003, Ralston’s life took a dramatic turn when he was trapped by a boulder in a remote Utah canyon. After five days of being stuck, he amputated his own arm with a dull pocketknife to free himself. This incident not only changed his life but also brought him international fame and recognition.

From Survival to Stardom
