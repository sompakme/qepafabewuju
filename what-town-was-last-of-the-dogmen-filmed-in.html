What is the origin of the term “Dog Soldiers”?

Dog Soldiers were named after a Cheyenne tale in which dogs change into formidable combatants. They wore big feather headdresses and bird-bone whistles around their necks, and they were armed with arrows and spears. The most elite Dog Soldiers wore “Dog Ropes,” which were sashes fashioned from buffalo hide and embellished with porcupine quills, feathers, and beads, which were worn around their waists.
