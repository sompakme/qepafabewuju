Candace Cameron-Bure Husband, Children, Family, Net Worth, Age, Height

Publish date: 2024-07-11

Following in the footsteps of her older brother, Candace Cameron-Bure dabbled in the entertainment industry and then shot to fame after her starring roles as DJ Tanner on tv family sitcom Full house which she took over as a DJ Tanner-Fuller on Fuller House.

As she began her career by starring in various commercials, Candace proved her prowess as an American actress, producer, writer, and talk show host. Currently, Who is the boss? , Growing Pains, Some Kind of Wonderful, Punchline, Boy Meets World, Make it or Break it and many more are some of the works she is renowned for. Indeed, she is a multi-talented woman.

Candace Cameron-Bure’s Bio (Age, Career)

The actress was born on April 6, 1976, in Panorama City, California, to a talent agent and the sister of actor Kirk Cameron.

Her professional career began at the age of 11 when she landed the role of DJ Tanner on Full House. The hit series ran for eight seasons with 192 episodes and finally came to an end in 1995, with Candace maintaining her position as an ensemble cast member for the entire series.

While filming the aforementioned series, Candace also appeared in several made-for-TV movies, including Nobody Would Tell She Cried No, NightScream as well as Camp Cucamonga and made her big screen debut with the feature film Punchlines. She hosted the Nickelodeon Kids’ Choice Awards with Dave Coulier and David Faustino in 1990, a role she reprized in 1994, making her the first person to host the awards on two or more occasions.

Candace Cameron-Bure also starred in a television series Cybill and Boy Meets World and in 1996. However, she took a break from television and film to focus on her family after having three children. Nonetheless, she did not entirely rely on her oars, as she continued to appear in movies and TV shows into the 2000s. She appeared as an interviewer on I Love the 80s and I Love the 80s. 80s Strikes Back and co-hosted 50 Cutest Kids: All Grown Up on the E! Network. She also had roles in the TV series It’s So Raven, The Betas well as the television movie Moonlight and Mistletoe for the Hallmark Channel in 2008. She then made her return to traditional acting with the television series Make or Break which ended in 2012.

In 2015, Candace announced that she would be reprising her role as DJ Tanner in the 2016 Netflix spin-off Fuller House, only this time her character would be renamed DJ Tanner-Fuller. She also competed on Dancing with the Stars and finished second in the week one rankings.

Apparently television isn’t her only forte as Candace has three books to her name; Reshaping Everything: Motivation for Physical and Spiritual Fitness, Balancing Everything: My Story of Priorities and Goals and Dancing Through Life: No Courage and Belief. One of the books turned out to be a New York Times bestseller.

Net value

Through her roles on Full House, as well as other hit shows and movies, Candace has amassed an estimated net worth of $12 million for herself.

Candace Cameron-Bure, husband, children, family

The actress married a Russian hockey player in the NHLValeri Bure in 1996, and their relationship can be described as stable. They first met at a charity hockey game where she was introduced to Dave Coulier, her co-star at Full House. The chemistry worked well between them and soon they walked down the aisle on June 22, 1996. Their union is lucky with three children – a daughter Natasha born in 1998 and two sons Lev born in 2000 and Maksim born in 2002.

Candace is the daughter of Robert Cameron and Barbara Cameron. She has three siblings named Kirk (an actor), Melissa and Bridgette Cameron.
